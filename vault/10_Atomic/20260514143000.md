## Core Idea

Stigmergy is a mechanism of **indirect coordination** where agents leave traces in
the environment that influence the behavior of other agents — without direct
communication between them.

## Origin

Coined by **Pierre-Paul Grassé in 1959** while studying termite colony behavior.
Termites don't coordinate by talking to each other; they deposit and respond to
pheromone trails in their shared environment, producing complex emergent structures.

## Pandora Instantiation

In Pandora, stigmergy manifests as **pheromone trails deposited on retrieval paths**
that successfully answer questions. Each successful retrieval reinforces those
edges, biasing future retrievals toward proven pathways — the vault learns from
its own usage patterns without explicit reprogramming.
